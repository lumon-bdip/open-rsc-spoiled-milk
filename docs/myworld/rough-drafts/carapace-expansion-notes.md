# Carapace Expansion Notes

Status: **rough draft for later design work**

Carapace armor should develop into the equipment family most closely associated
with poison. Its identity should include both using poison offensively and
surviving poison better than other armor families. This is an early direction,
not a finished balance or implementation plan.

## Existing Starting Point

The current full-set traits establish three combat-style poison routes:

- Scorpion carapace applies poison through melee attacks.
- Spider carapace applies poison through ranged attacks.
- Magic-spider carapace applies poison through magic attacks and removes the
  normal leather/carapace Magic Power penalty.

These sets are a useful foundation, but carapaces should eventually do more
than proc poison on enemies.

## Intended Family Identity

Each carapace family should combine two related traits:

1. An offensive poison behavior suited to its creature and combat style.
2. A defensive poison-mitigation behavior that helps the wearer resist,
   contain, or recover from poison.

Possible mitigation mechanics to explore later include:

- reducing poison power when poison is first applied;
- limiting the maximum poison power that can accumulate;
- reducing the chance that an incoming poison effect succeeds;
- increasing poison decay while the set is worn;
- reducing poison tick damage without granting complete immunity; or
- giving certain sets a conditional cleanse after a defined trigger.

Different carapace sets do not all need the same mitigation mechanic. Their
defensive behavior can reflect the source creature, which gives the family
variety while retaining a recognizable poison theme.

The design should avoid making antidotes obsolete or duplicating Nature
amulets exactly. Potions can remain the reliable route to temporary immunity,
while Nature amulets retain their general poison-decay identity. Carapaces can
instead emphasize combat-facing resistance, poison-cap control, or narrower
creature-specific defenses.

## Enemy And Equipment Expansion

The carapace family needs more bug-themed enemies so it can support meaningful
progression rather than stopping with scorpions and spiders. Candidate creature
families to investigate include:

- beetles or scarabs, emphasizing armor and poison resistance;
- wasps or hornets, emphasizing fast poison application;
- centipedes, emphasizing stronger or stacking venom;
- mantises, emphasizing melee pressure;
- ants, emphasizing swarms or group-combat effects; and
- higher-tier magical or corrupted variants of existing insects.

These are theme candidates only. Later planning should first audit available
NPC sprites, animations, map space, combat levels, crafting materials, and
existing enemy niches before deciding which creatures become real content.

## Questions For A Later Pass

- Should poison mitigation require the full five-piece set, or should each
  individual carapace piece provide a smaller defensive benefit?
- Should each set protect primarily against its own poison style, or should all
  carapaces provide broadly useful poison defense?
- How should carapace crafting and progression connect to creature drops?
- Which mitigation mechanics remain useful without invalidating antidotes,
  Nature equipment, or poison-focused enemies?
- Do higher-tier sets strengthen existing poison, unlock new poison behavior,
  or trade offense for stronger mitigation?
